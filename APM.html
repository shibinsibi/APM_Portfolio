What I Learned

People downloaded VitaFit excited to get fit — and quietly disappeared after 2 weeks. Nobody knew why. Everyone assumed they wanted more workout options. I dug deeper.

They didn't want more workouts. They wanted someone to do it with. That's when JTBD clicked for me — stop asking what users do, start asking why.

Case Study Applied Here

Why Are Users Leaving VitaFit?

VitaFit · Fitness App · Retention Strategy
PRD Written

The Problem

Users churning after 2 weeks. Solo workouts felt isolating. No social hook keeping people accountable.

JTBD Applied

Users don't want fitness — they want to feel seen. Community was the hidden gap behind the retention metric.

Outcome

PRD: "Buddy Streaks" & "Trainer Circles." KPIs defined. Gamified social accountability flows designed.

JTBDPRD WritingJourney MappingKPI DefinitionFigma
💡

Key Takeaway

"Motivation is social. Before you build a feature — ask what job the user is actually hiring this product to do."

Case Study Applied Here

The Hidden Fee Killing Zomato's Retention

Zomato · Food Delivery · Prioritization
RICE Applied

The Problem

High abandonment at checkout. Users dropping at the most critical moment — trust broken right before conversion.

RICE Applied

"Hidden Fees" = #1 abandonment driver. Transparent Pricing scored highest: max Reach × Impact, lowest Effort.

Outcome

"Transparent Pricing" as clear #1 priority. Trust fixed first — UX second. Data-backed decision.

RICEMoSCoWCompetitive ResearchRetention Analysis
💡

Key Takeaway

"Users don't abandon bad products. They abandon broken trust. Research first — scoring second."

Case Study Applied Here

Killing Decision Fatigue for Busy Professionals

YummiWeek · Meal Planning · MVP Validation
MVP Built

The Problem

Busy professionals face decision fatigue around meals. Too many choices — zero connection to what they already had at home.

How I Validated

Built MVP in Lovable. Tested nutritional auto-calculator with real users before a single engineering sprint was planned.

Outcome

High WTP validated. Friction reduced by matching pantry to recipes — concept proven before real code was written.

LovableMVP DesignUser TestingWTP Analysis

Key Takeaway

What I Learned

Growth isn't just acquiring users — it's maximising value from every interaction they already have. And in luxury e-commerce, trust is the growth lever nobody talks about.

After 3 years watching high-value transactions, I know: at premium price points, the upsell only works when the user already trusts you completely.

Case Study Applied Here

How Do You Make Bundles Feel Personal on Amazon?

Amazon · E-commerce · AOV Growth
Feature Proposed

The Problem

Generic bundles felt like a push — low trust, low relevance. AOV left on the table at every checkout interaction.

JTBD Applied

Users resist irrelevant upsells, not upsells themselves. "Build Your Own Bundle" makes personalisation the upsell.

Outcome

Passive browsing becomes active personalisation — AOV and trust improved simultaneously. Zero pressure.

JTBDAOV StrategyCompetitive AnalysisFeature Design
💡

Key Takeaway

"People don't resist upsells. They resist irrelevant ones. Trust is the highest-converting growth strategy in e-commerce."
